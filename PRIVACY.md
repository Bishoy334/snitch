# Snitch — Privacy Policy

Snitch does not collect, store, transmit, or sell any user data. Period.

Everything Snitch shows you — trackers, fingerprinting attempts, cookies,
storage, the cross-site ledger — is computed on your device, using Chrome
extension APIs, against a tracker database bundled inside the extension.
Snitch makes no network requests of its own. There is no server, no
account, no analytics, no telemetry, and no crash reporting.

Data Snitch keeps locally on your device (and nowhere else):

- Per-tab inspection reports (cleared when the tab closes).
- A local ledger of which tracking companies appeared on which sites, so
  Snitch can tell you "Google has seen you on N other sites." Entries
  expire after 30 days. This never leaves your browser.
- Your settings (for example, sites flagged "forget when I close").

Clearing the extension's storage (chrome://extensions → Snitch → site
data) or uninstalling removes all of it.

The only network activity Snitch ever causes is when you click an
external link in the popup (for example, a tracker company's homepage or
the developer's Buy Me a Coffee page), which opens in a normal browser
tab.
